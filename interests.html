Interests

& Miscellaneous

Outside of work and school, I enjoy cycling, gaming, messing around with crochet, and occasionally reading way too much about data visualization theory. I also enjoy volunteering and doing my best to support others - I've highlighted some of this work on this page.

P.S. I also adopted a shelter dog in 2020, he's great, his name is Tank.

Cycling

As a kid I enjoyed biking around the Midwest forest paths, often old railroad lines converted to trails. Biking was a way to spend energy and enjoy outside. Then at University of Illinois my bike became my main form of transportation and I leaned into the bike culture. I became a shop mechanic for a University affiliated bicycle co-op, where I learned basic bicycle repair and participated in cycling infrastructure advocacy. I learned about urban planning from the bicyclist’s point of view.

Bike Mechanic

University of Illinois · Urbana-Champaign
  • Mechanic and shop assistant for a university affiliated bicycle workshop for residents and students
  • Provided mechanical assistance on bicycles in an educational and directional manner, working with members
  • Participated in bike friendly programs and sustainable transportation advocacy
May 2015 - December 2015
It was particularly poignant for me as a studying civil engineer to see how cycling infrastructure is valued (and undervalued) in America. It was easy to see how difficult the infrastructure makes it for people to safely get around. It was interesting that this varies across cities in the US, and contrasts with cities in Europe. Cycling was a way I could think about engineering in different contexts, and to personalize the concept of seeing the needs of different users in a system.
Cycling continues to be a great source of destressing for me. I’ve slowed down some since my bike mechanic days, but I still enjoy supporting bike infrastructure groups.
I have attempted mountain biking. I can say I love riding the smooth, earthy, even sometimes “loggy” forest trails of the Midwest, but I haven’t quite figured out the rocks and spikes in the Southwest. Still pending.

Volunteering

General notes:
Volunteering has been a great way for me to explore spaces in the community I wouldn't otherwise get from work or school. I've learned so much about teamwork and the business side of spaces I care about - charity work, habitat restoration, urban planning. These are some of my most impactful volunteering experiences:

Games Done Quick · Remote

Event volunteer: donations processing and Twitch moderation

I enjoy video games, and supporting charities, so I was pretty excited to be able to support a charity based on playing video games! Games Done Quick is something of a niche internet phenomenon, where people showcase different gaming strategies on Twitch.com. I’ve followed it for a few years as a viewer, but I was thrilled for the opportunity to volunteer for the all-women “Frame Fatales” events. I assisted with processing live donations on the Games Done Quick website for events, and for live Twitch chat moderation during the event. These are relatively small volunteer positions, but it was amazing to be one of many women working together to get the whole show running. The Frame Fatale events support Malala Fund, a charity that aims to give every young woman access to education.

August 2022 &
February 2023

Duck Creek Cleanup · Davenport, IA

Habitat restoration / parks cleaup volunteer

Duck Creek is a waterway that goes through the Quad City metro area and the Parks department of Davenport, IA created a program to help take care of it. I mainly assisted with picking up trash around parks, with some habitat restoration work (removing invasive species). It was neat to be a volunteer while working with local government, and to see the kinds of programs a Parks and Rec department puts together. I also greatly enjoyed working with other volunteers who care about keeping the parks clean.

May 2018 - April 2019

The Morton Arboretum · Glen Ellyn, IL

Event volunteer

The Morton Arboretum is primarily a tree museum and research facility, but it serves as a community space too. I have assisted with multiple community events there: a 5-k run, outdoor theater events, orchestral concerts, art markets, and a cider-tasting festival, all to support the arboretum’s grounds and programs. I learned a lot about group coordination from the event leaders and a little bit about how a large tree research institution operates. This was the largest public garden near where I grew up and it was pretty inspiring to me as a nature-loving kid. It was wonderful to give back to this space as an adult, and to work with a variety of people all connected by loving the space.
