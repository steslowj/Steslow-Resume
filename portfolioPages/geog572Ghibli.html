Mapbox allows users to create custom styles to geographic and political data in the application "Mapbox Studio."
This style is inspired by the colorful fantasy animated films made by Studio Ghibli - examples: Spirited Away (2001), Castle in the Sky (1986), My Neighbor Totoro (1988), Howl's Moving Castle (2004). I tried to emulate the bright and saturated color palettes of the films. I focused on highlighting variations in terrain, emphasizing parks and natural features over commercial features, while still calling attention to cultural centers - Ghibli films are as much about people and places as they are about nature. I chose mostly simple fonts like in Ghibli film titles and merchandising, but with a little bit of fantasy and an animated feel. The airports are highlighted in red in reference to the film Porco Rosso (1992) and Studio Ghibli's general love of airplanes. Zoom in on the little Totoros for some info on settings that inspired Ghibli films!
